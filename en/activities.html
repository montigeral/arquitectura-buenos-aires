Cultural Experiences

Buenos Aires is a city renowned for its cultural, historical, and architectural wealth. Its avenues, squares, and buildings reflect the influence of different European styles that arrived in the country between the 19th and 20th centuries.

Through these cultural activities, visitors can discover the history and heritage value of some of the city's most emblematic buildings by participating in guided tours, photography workshops, and visits to unique architectural spaces.

Historical Tours

Guided walking tours along Avenida de Mayo, one of the city's most representative avenues, known for its European-inspired architecture.

During the tour, you will visit historic buildings, traditional cafes, and palaces that are part of the cultural heritage of Buenos Aires.

Photographic Safaris

Activities designed for those who enjoy urban photography and want to capture the architectural details of the city.

During the tour, elements of Art Deco, eclecticism, and other styles present in historic buildings of Buenos Aires are analyzed.

Domes Route

Buenos Aires is known as the city of domes, as it has more than 300 such structures distributed across different neighborhoods.

This tour allows access to viewpoints and terraces from where you can appreciate panoramic views of the city and learn about the history of these constructions.

Special Events and Activities

Throughout the year, various cultural events related to the city's architectural heritage are held, promoting its conservation and dissemination.

Heritage Nights

During this cultural event, several historic buildings open their doors to the public, allowing access to interior spaces that are not normally available for visits.

Talks and Conferences

Meetings with architects and historians who explain the evolution of the architecture of Buenos Aires and the importance of preserving its urban heritage.

Cultural Exhibitions

Temporary exhibitions featuring photographs, historic blueprints, and scale models showcasing the evolution of the city's most representative buildings.
